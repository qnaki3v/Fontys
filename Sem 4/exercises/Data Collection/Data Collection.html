Loading and Previewing GDP Data

In this cell, we:

  1. Import necessary libraries: pandas for data handling and matplotlib.pyplot for visualization.
  2. Load three datasets:
    • real_gdp_growth.xls: Contains real GDP growth data (annual % change).
    • gdp_current_prices.xls: Contains GDP in current prices (billion USD).
    • gdp_per_capita.xls: Contains GDP per capita (USD).

Visualizing Bulgaria's GDP Data

Steps Performed:

  1. Data Cleaning:

    • Removed empty rows and unnecessary columns.
    • Converted indices to integer years.

  2. Data Transformation:

    • Reformatted the datasets into DataFrames with appropriate labels:
      • real_gdp_growth: Annual GDP growth rates (%).
      • gdp_current_prices: GDP in billion USD.
      • gdp_per_capita: GDP per capita in USD.

  3. Data Visualization:

    • Line Plot: GDP at current prices over the years.
    • Bar Chart: Real GDP growth rate.
    • Line Plot: GDP per capita over the years.

Generated Plots:

  1. Bulgaria GDP at Current Prices (Billions USD)

    • Line plot with markers to visualize GDP trends over the years.

  2. Bulgaria Real GDP Growth Rate (%)

    • Bar chart to show yearly GDP growth fluctuations.

  3. Bulgaria GDP per Capita (USD)

    • Line plot illustrating GDP per capita changes over time.

real_gdp_growth_path = 'real_gdp_growth.xls'
gdp_current_prices_path = 'gdp_current_prices.xls'
gdp_per_capita_path = 'gdp_per_capita.xls'

real_gdp_growth = pd.read_excel(real_gdp_growth_path, engine='xlrd')
gdp_current_prices = pd.read_excel(gdp_current_prices_path, engine='xlrd')
gdp_per_capita = pd.read_excel(gdp_per_capita_path, engine='xlrd')

real_gdp_growth = real_gdp_growth.drop(real_gdp_growth.columns[0], axis=1).dropna(how='all')
gdp_current_prices = gdp_current_prices.drop(gdp_current_prices.columns[0], axis=1).dropna(how='all')
gdp_per_capita = gdp_per_capita.drop(gdp_per_capita.columns[0], axis=1).dropna(how='all')

years = real_gdp_growth.columns.astype(int)
real_gdp_growth = pd.DataFrame(real_gdp_growth.iloc[0].values, index=years, columns=['Real GDP Growth'])
gdp_current_prices = pd.DataFrame(gdp_current_prices.iloc[0].values, index=years, columns=['GDP (Billion USD)'])
gdp_per_capita = pd.DataFrame(gdp_per_capita.iloc[0].values, index=years, columns=['GDP per Capita (USD)'])

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))
plt.plot(gdp_current_prices, marker='o', linestyle='-')
plt.xlabel("Year")
plt.ylabel("GDP (Billion USD)")
plt.title("Bulgaria GDP at Current Prices (Billions USD)")
plt.grid()
plt.show()

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))
plt.bar(real_gdp_growth.index, real_gdp_growth['Real GDP Growth'], color='b')

plt.title("Bulgaria Real GDP Growth Rate")
plt.grid()
plt.show()

GDP vs. GDP per Capita Over Time

Key Features of the Plot:

  1. Left Y-Axis (Blue, Circles, Dashed Line)

    • Represents GDP in Billion USD.
    • Shows overall GDP growth trends.

  2. Right Y-Axis (Orange, Squares, Dashed Line)

    • Represents GDP per Capita (USD).
    • Indicates how GDP per capita evolved alongside total GDP.

Insights from the Graph:

  • A sharp decline in GDP and GDP per capita around 1990, likely due to economic transitions post-socialism.
  • Gradual increase post-2000, with rapid acceleration post-2010, indicating economic growth.
  • Both metrics show a strong correlation, meaning GDP growth is effectively translating into higher income per person.

fig, ax1 = plt.subplots(figsize=(10, 5))

ax1.set_xlabel("Year")
ax1.set_ylabel("GDP (Billion USD)", color='blue')
ax1.plot(gdp_current_prices.index, gdp_current_prices.iloc[:, 0], label="GDP (Billion USD)", color='blue', marker='o', linestyle='-')
ax1.tick_params(axis='y', labelcolor='blue')

ax2 = ax1.twinx()
ax2.set_ylabel("GDP per Capita (USD)", color='orange')
ax2.plot(gdp_per_capita.index, gdp_per_capita.iloc[:, 0], label="GDP per Capita (USD)", color='orange', marker='s', linestyle='--')
ax2.tick_params(axis='y', labelcolor='orange')

plt.title("GDP vs. GDP per Capita Over Time")
fig.tight_layout()
plt.show()

    • Represents Real GDP Growth (%).

Insights from the Graph:
